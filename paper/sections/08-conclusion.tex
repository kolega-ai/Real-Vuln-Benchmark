\section{Conclusion}

We introduced RealVuln Benchmark, the first fully open-source SAST evaluation
framework grounded in real-world, intentionally vulnerable code.  Version 1
spans 26 repositories, 796 manually labeled findings, and 120 false-positive
traps, evaluated against 15 scanners drawn from both the traditional static
analysis ecosystem and the emerging class of LLM-based security tools.  Every
artifact --- ground truth, raw scanner output, scoring pipeline, dashboard
generator, and evaluation prompts --- is released under a permissive license
with a reproducible manifest that version-locks results to exact benchmark
snapshots.

Our experiments reveal a striking capability gap.  LLM-based scanners achieve
F3 scores (strict) 2--4$\times$ higher than their traditional counterparts: the best
pure-LLM system, Claude Sonnet 4.6 operating with agentic tool use, reaches an
F3 of 51.7, compared with 17.7 for Semgrep, the top-performing traditional tool on F3.
The hybrid system Kolega leads the overall benchmark at F3\,=\,73.0 (strict).
Agentic tool use is itself a substantial differentiator --- multi-turn agents
consistently outperform single-turn prompted LLMs on the same underlying model.
Nevertheless, no scanner dominates across all CWE families: traditional tools
retain a precision advantage on well-specified injection patterns, while LLMs
excel on logic-level and authentication flaws that require broader code context.
The FP-trap analysis further reveals that several high-recall systems achieve
their scores at the cost of elevated false-positive rates, underscoring the
importance of a strongly recall-weighted metric like F3 that still penalises noise.

The benchmark is designed to evolve.  A community contribution pathway,
described in \S\ref{sec:living-benchmark}, allows new repositories, scanners,
and ground-truth corrections to be incorporated without invalidating prior
results.  Version 2 will expand coverage to JavaScript/TypeScript, Go, and
Java, and will introduce Type 2 targets drawn from real production CVEs ---
setting a higher bar for precision in realistic, low-vulnerability-density
codebases.  We invite the security research community to submit scanner results,
propose new repositories, and challenge existing labels.

All code, data, results, and tooling are available at
\url{https://github.com/kolega-ai/RealVulnBenchmark}.
