How do security scanners perform on real-world code? We present \textsc{RealVuln}, the first open-source benchmark comparing Rule-Based SAST, general-purpose LLMs, and Security-Specialized scanners on 26 real-world Python repositories with 796 hand-labeled entries (676 vulnerabilities, 120 false-positive traps). We test 15 scanners (3~Rule-Based SAST, 10~general-purpose LLM, 2~Security-Specialized) and rank them by F3 score ($\beta{=}3$, weighting recall 9$\times$ over precision). A clear three-tier ranking emerges. The Security-Specialized scanner Kolega.Dev (F3\,=\,73.0) outperforms the best general-purpose LLM, Claude Sonnet~4.6 (F3\,=\,51.7), which in turn scores nearly 3$\times$ higher than the best Rule-Based tool, Semgrep (F3\,=\,17.7). All code, ground-truth data, scanner outputs, and scoring scripts are released under an open-source license. An interactive dashboard is at \url{https://realvuln.kolega.dev/}. \textsc{RealVuln} is a living benchmark: versioned, community-driven, with a roadmap toward multi-language coverage.
